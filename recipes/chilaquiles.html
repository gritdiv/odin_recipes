Green Chilaquiles

Delicious, comforting green chilaquiles are easy enough to make with pre-made, store-bought ingredients like tortilla chips and a jar of salsa. But to make your chilaquiles really memorable, follow this recipe to make them from scratch.

To make green chilaquiles that are abuela-worthy, the secret is in making a homemade, brothy green salsa. You make everything from scratch, right down to the chicken broth - no tetrapak! You'll shred the chicken to top your chilaquiles with the same chicken breast you use to make your stock. Then fire up your blender because you're going to make a fresh green salsa bursting with flavor (which is easier than you think). And finally, you're going to cut up day-old corn tortillas into triangles and fry them up golden brown to make the bed for your chilaquiles. Scarf them down while they're still hot!

Ingredients

Green Salsa

  • Homemade chicken stock (Click here for recipe)
  • 1/2 kg tomatillos aka tomates verdes
  • 1/4 piece of white onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1-2 serrano chiles, whole
  • 1 bunch cilantro, washed
  • Salt and sugar to taste

Chilaquiles Bed and Toppings

  • 12 day-old corn tortillas (preferably made with nixtamalized corn)
  • 1 whole, skinless, bone-in chicken breast
  • Mexican cream
  • dry, crumbly cheese like ranchero
  • (optional)white onion slices
  • (optional)cilantro sprigs for garnish

Steps

  1. If you haven't done so already, make your chicken stock.
  2. Boil together your tomatillos, garlic, 1/4 onion, bunch of cilantro, and chiles until the color turns from bright green to a dull, yellowish green.
  3. Transfer all the boiled ingredients to a blender, along with 1/2 of chicken broth. Add sugar and salt to taste.
  4. Blend.
  5. Add cooking oil to a large saucepan and heat on medium flame.
  6. Transfer the blended green salsa to the saucepan and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Shred your chicken breast meat.
  8. Cut day-old tortillas into triangles.
  9. In a saut√© pan, add oil and salt.
  10. Fry up the tortilla triangles until golden brown.
  11. Plate up your chilaquiles. Ladel on top a generous amount of green salsa and shredded chicken.
  12. Garnish with cream, crumbled cheese, and onion and cilantro sprigs if desired.
